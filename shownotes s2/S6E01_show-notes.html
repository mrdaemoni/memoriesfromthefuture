S6E01 — The Sound of Your Frame Breaking

Season 6: The Koans You Carry
Season 6, Episode 1 Origin: Hector (2026-04-15)

The Koan

"The deepest ignorance is not what you fail to know but the frame you do not know you are inside."

Supporting Synthesis

"Mu is the sound of a mind that has outgrown its own frame."

Episode Summary

The season opens with its most fundamental question — what happens when you can't see the thing that's shaping everything you see? The frame is the cognitive architecture you inhabit without knowing it. Pirsig tracked systems of ideas, not ideas. McGilchrist showed the left hemisphere forgets it serves something larger. This episode maps the invisible walls and asks what it takes to hear them crack.

Thinkers Featured

Gregory Bateson

20th c. — Cybernetics / Anthropology

Logical types, Learning I vs Learning II. Most suffering is people trying to solve Level 2 problems with Level 1 tools. "Learning to learn" — not learning a new thing, but learning to see the level your learning is happening on.

Thomas Kuhn

20th c. — Philosophy of Science

Paradigm shifts, normal science vs revolutionary science. The frame doesn't break incrementally; it snaps.

Shunryu Suzuki

20th c. — Zen Buddhism

"In the beginner's mind there are many possibilities. In the expert's mind there are few." The Rinzai tradition's daigi (great doubt) as technology for breaking frames.

Mark Beeman & John Kounios

21st c. — Neuroscience

The insight moment. The gamma burst that happens when a problem suddenly opens. The brain quiets visual input and lets the anterior superior temporal gyrus fire — a neural frame-break.

Iain McGilchrist

20th–21st c. — Psychiatry / Philosophy

Left hemisphere as the emissary that forgets it serves something larger. The map that convinces itself it is the territory.
